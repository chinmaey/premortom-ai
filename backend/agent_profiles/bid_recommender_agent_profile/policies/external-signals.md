# External Market And Specification Signals

External research should be structured before it reaches the recommender.

Useful signals include:

- Market price range.
- Typical delivery timeline.
- Typical advance payment or retention practice.
- Typical warranty start trigger.
- Installation and commissioning responsibility norms.
- Training inclusion norms.
- Service SLA expectations.
- Certification or regulatory expectations.
- Vendor/product reputation and adverse-news signals.

Every market/specification claim should include source metadata such as URL,
retrieval timestamp, and confidence or quality notes.

Use external signals to identify unusual terms or missing information. Do not
treat external signals as current quote evidence.
