# Warranty Review Guidance

## Rule

Warranty should normally begin at commissioning, acceptance, or operational handover, not merely at delivery, when buyer-side readiness can delay use.

## Risk Signals

- Warranty starts on delivery while installation or site readiness remains buyer-dependent.
- Warranty duration is short relative to expected commissioning delays.
- Warranty exclusions are broad or unclear.
- Acceptance testing is missing or disconnected from warranty start.

## Follow-Up Questions

- When does warranty start: delivery, installation, commissioning, acceptance, or first clinical/operational use?
- Who absorbs warranty time lost due to site-readiness delays?
- Are replacement parts, preventive maintenance, and labor included?
